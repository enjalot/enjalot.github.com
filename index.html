I help world-class product teams ship complex software by prototyping interfaces for deep data problems.

At Observable I put my 10 years of data visualization experience towards developing and demonstrating advanced data workflows. I've taught thousands of data practitioners via video, live workshops and interative learning materials.

At Google I spent 4 years on the design team prototyping and developing data visualization tools to better understand the 100+ products in Google Cloud. I collaborated with members of the Google Brain team to create interactive research publications.

A the first hire at Lever I designed & developed the original data infrastructure, product features and internal tools. Many tools and data patterns that I designed were still in use when Lever was acquired for $500m.

Open Source, Open Science & Open Data

Distill was a scientific journal for publishing interactive Machine learning research. I co-authored How to Use t-SNE Effectively, Four Experiments in Handwriting with a Neural Network, The Building Blocks of Interpretability, and Activation Atlas

Wrote Introducing the Quick, Draw! Dataset, working with the Google Creative Lab to open source the dataset and with Google Brain to demo the SketchRNN model. I gave a talk at OpenVisConf about what I learned in Machine Learning for Visualization.

Block Builder was an in-browser editor for quickly making, editing, remixing and sharing d3.js code examples. Launched via Kickstarter it supported the curation of more than 40,000 community examples.

I love collecting, exploring and visualizing data! I started the Observable Curated Datasets project, and have worked on Wildfire data, Air quality data, Emoji data, COVID data, and Election data. I've analyzed data on medieval monks with my father by making Tremulator and explored how typing in Chinese works with Pinyin.

Part of my process is building and sharing small experiments. I've got hundreds of d3.js blocks, Observable notebooks and collaborations demonstrating various data visualization, UX and graphics techniques.

Community Leadership
Learning complex technology is difficult, and I believe it is always better to learn together.

Co-founded and co-organized the Bay Area d3.js meetup in 2012 which has grown to 5,000+ members in person and 9,000+ online. Wrote several popular articles on D3 including Touring the d3.js ecosystem, Hitchhiker's Guide to d3.js, The Trouble with D3, and How do you learn d3.js?

Co-founded and co-organized the d3.js unconference for 5 years. Each year a 100+ top d3.js practitioners gathered to discuss, learn and advance d3.js together.
